1. What does uname -r show before your new kernel?

Before booting into the newly compiled kernel, uname -r shows the version of the currently running kernel that came with your distribution.
$uname -r
The exact version depends on your system.

2. What does it show after?
After successfully booting into the newly compiled kernel, uname -r should display the version of the kernel you built and installed.
if you configured a custom local version string.


3. Did anything stop working (WiFi, sound, etc.)?

After booting the new kernel, verify that major hardware and services are functioning correctly:
WiFi: Check network connectivity.
ip a
ping google.com
Sound: Play an audio file or use:
aplay /usr/share/sounds/alsa/Front_Center.wav
Bluetooth: Verify adapters are detected.
bluetoothctl list
USB Devices: Confirm USB devices are recognized.
lsusb
Graphics/Display: Ensure the desktop loads properly and screen resolution is correct.
Loaded Modules: Check whether required modules are loaded.
lsmod
Record any issues you observe, such as:
WiFi adapter not detected.
No sound output.
Bluetooth unavailable.
USB devices not recognized.
Graphics driver problems or low resolution.
Boot warnings or errors in dmesg.

If everything works normally, note that the new kernel booted successfully and all major peripherals are functioning as expected.
